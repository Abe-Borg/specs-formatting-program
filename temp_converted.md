# SECTION 21 13 13
  WET-PIPE SPRINKLER SYSTEMS

## PART 1 - GENERAL

### 1.01	SUMMARY
- A.	Section Includes:
  - 1.	Wet-pipe fire sprinkler system.
  - 2.	Sprinkler piping, fittings, and hangers.
  - 3.	Sprinkler heads.
  - 4.	Alarm valves and accessories.
- B.	Related Requirements:
  - 1.	Section 21 05 00 - Common Work Results for Fire Suppression.
  - 2.	Section 21 05 29 - Hangers and Supports for Fire-Suppression Piping and Equipment.

### 1.02	REFERENCES
- A.	NFPA 13-2022 - Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems.
- B.	ASTM A53/A53M - Standard Specification for Pipe, Steel, Black and Hot-Dipped, Zinc-Coated, Welded and Seamless.
- C.	UL 199 - Automatic Sprinklers for Fire-Protection Service.

### 1.03	SUBMITTALS
- A.	Product Data: Submit manufacturer's technical data for each sprinkler type, valve, and fitting.
- B.	Shop Drawings:
  - 1.	Sprinkler system layout showing pipe sizes, sprinkler locations, and hanger locations.
  - 2.	Hydraulic calculations stamped by a licensed fire protection engineer.
  - 3.	Seismic bracing calculations and layout.
- C.	Closeout Submittals:
  - 1.	Operation and maintenance manuals.
  - 2.	Contractor's Material and Test Certificate per NFPA 13.

### 1.04	QUALITY ASSURANCE
- A.	Installer Qualifications: Licensed C-16 Fire Protection Contractor in the State of California with minimum 5 years experience on similar K-12 projects.
- B.	Design Responsibility: Delegated design by fire protection engineer licensed in California.
- C.	Regulatory Requirements: Comply with DSA (Division of the State Architect) requirements.

## PART 2 - PRODUCTS

### 2.01	PIPE AND FITTINGS
- A.	Steel Pipe: ASTM A53/A53M, Grade B, Schedule 40, black steel.
- B.	Fittings:
  - 1.	Threaded: Cast-iron or malleable-iron, 150 psi rated.
  - 2.	Grooved: Ductile-iron or steel, listed for fire protection service.

### 2.02	SPRINKLER HEADS
- A.	General Requirements:
  - 1.	UL listed and FM approved.
  - 2.	Brass construction with appropriate finish for location.
  - 3.	Temperature rating per NFPA 13 Table 6.2.5.1.
- B.	Types:
  - 1.	Concealed pendent: K-factor 5.6, quick-response, white cover plate.
  - 2.	Upright: K-factor 5.6, standard-response, chrome finish.
  - 3.	Sidewall: K-factor 5.6, quick-response, horizontal discharge.

### 2.03	VALVES
- A.	Alarm Check Valve: UL listed, FM approved, complete with trim, retard chamber, and alarm devices.
- B.	Control Valves: OS&Y gate valves, UL listed, with tamper switch.

## PART 3 - EXECUTION

### 3.01	EXAMINATION
- A.	Verify building structural elements are suitable for hanger attachment.
- B.	Confirm ceiling heights and types match design documents.
- C.	Do not proceed until unsatisfactory conditions are corrected.

### 3.02	INSTALLATION
- A.	Install in accordance with NFPA 13-2022 and manufacturer's instructions.
- B.	Piping:
  - 1.	Slope branch lines toward drain points minimum 1/4 inch per 10 feet.
  - 2.	Provide seismic bracing per NFPA 13 and California Building Code.
  - 3.	Maintain clearances from electrical equipment per NEC.
- C.	Sprinkler Heads:
  - 1.	Install perpendicular to ceiling within 1/8-inch tolerance.
  - 2.	Maintain deflector-to-ceiling distances per NFPA 13 Table 8.6.4.1.1.
  - 3.	Coordinate concealed head cover plate color with Architect.

### 3.03	FIELD QUALITY CONTROL
- A.	Hydrostatic Test:
  - 1.	Test at 200 psi for 2 hours.
  - 2.	No leakage or pressure drop permitted.
  - 3.	Repair leaks and retest until satisfactory.
- B.	Flush piping before installation of alarm devices and sprinkler heads.
- C.	Demonstrate alarm operation to Owner and Fire Marshal.

### 3.04	CLEANING
- A.	Remove construction debris from sprinkler system components.
- B.	Clean exposed piping and equipment of dirt, fingerprints, and labels.

### 3.05	DEMONSTRATION
- A.	Train Owner's maintenance personnel on system operation, including:
  - 1.	Alarm valve operation and testing procedures.
  - 2.	Inspector's test connection use.
  - 3.	Sprinkler head replacement procedures.
- B.	Provide minimum 2 hours of training.
